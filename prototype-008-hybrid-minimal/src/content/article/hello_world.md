## Why Minimal?

There is a quiet confidence in restraint. The best editorial designs — from early print magazines to modern platforms like Medium — understand that content is the product. Everything else is scaffolding.

This portfolio follows that philosophy. No hero images fighting for attention. No sidebar widgets begging for clicks. Just words, set in clean serif type against generous whitespace.

## The Design Principles

**Typography first.** The typeface carries the voice. A well-chosen serif font with proper line height and measure makes reading effortless.

**Narrow column.** The content column is capped at 550 pixels. This is not a limitation — it is a deliberate choice rooted in decades of typographic research on optimal reading width.

**Monochrome.** A simple palette of dark text on a light background. Every element earns its place through content, not color.

## What Comes Next

This is a starting point. Articles will cover technology, design thinking, and the creative process. Videos will document the journey. Links will connect the dots.

The goal is not to impress with flashy animations or complex layouts. The goal is to communicate clearly and let the work speak for itself.
